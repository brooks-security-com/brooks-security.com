10|## Purpose
11|
12|This policy specifies the acceptable use of end-user computing devices and technology provided by ____. It also establishes training requirements to ensure understanding of current best practices, data sensitivity classifications, and the consequences of non-compliance.
13|
14|## Scope
15|
16|This policy applies to all Personnel who use organizational computing devices, networks, or information systems, whether organization-owned or personally-owned devices used for business purposes (BYOD).
17|
18|## Policy
19|
20|____ requires that:
21|
22|- Background verification checks on all candidates for employment and contracting must be carried out in accordance with relevant laws, regulations, and ethics, and proportional to the business requirements, the classification of the information to be accessed, and the perceived risk.
23|- Personnel must agree to and sign the terms and conditions of the "Employee Access and Confidentiality Agreement" and comply with acceptable use requirements.
24|- Personnel must complete an onboarding process that familiarizes them with the environments, systems, security requirements, and procedures. Personnel must also complete ongoing security awareness training that is audited.
25|- Employee offboarding must include reiterating duties and responsibilities still valid after termination, verifying removal of access to systems, and ensuring return of all organization-owned assets.
26|- Personnel must take reasonable measures to ensure no sensitive or confidential data is transmitted via unencrypted digital communications or posted on public social media.
27|- The organization documents a list of prohibited activities in the Employee Access and Confidentiality Agreement.
28|- A fair disciplinary process will be utilized for Personnel suspected of committing security breaches.
29|
30|## Acceptable Use Requirements
31|
32|All Personnel must comply with the following acceptable use requirements:
33|
34|- All Personnel are considered ____ (remote/on-site/hybrid) users and must follow all system access controls and procedures applicable to their work arrangement.
35|- Use of organizational computing systems is subject to monitoring by ____ (IT/Security) teams.
36|- Personnel must not leave computing devices used for business purposes, including organization-provided and BYOD devices, unattended in public.
37|- Device encryption must be enabled for all mobile and desktop devices accessing organizational data, such as full-disk encryption.
38|- Personnel are required to use only organization-owned, managed, and configured devices for local handling of customer and sensitive data.
39|- All electronic messages containing sensitive or confidential data must be encrypted.
40|- Personnel must not post sensitive or confidential data in public forums or chat rooms. If posting is needed for technical support, data must be sanitized to remove sensitive information prior to posting.
41|- All data storage devices and media must be managed according to the Data Classification Policy and Data Handling procedures.
42|
43|## Protection Against Malware
44|
45|### Restrictions on Software Installation
46|
47|- Only legal, approved software with a valid license must be used. Use of personal software for business purposes and vice versa is prohibited.
48|- The principle of least privilege must be applied for software installation rights.
49|- The organization must identify which types of software installations are permitted or prohibited.
50|- Anti-malware or equivalent protection and monitoring must be installed and enabled on all endpoint systems that may be affected by malware, including workstations, laptops, and servers.
51|- Vulnerabilities that could be exploited by malware must be reduced through technical vulnerability management.
52|
53|### Malware Protection
54|
55|All endpoint systems must be protected by anti-malware or EDR (Endpoint Detection and Response) software with automatic updates and real-time scanning enabled. Malware detection must be implemented at multiple points: email gateways, web proxies, endpoints, cloud storage, and removable media interfaces.
56|
57|Software installation must follow the principle of least privilege, and only legally licensed, approved software may be used on organizational equipment.
58|

63|Violation of this policy may result in disciplinary action as outlined in the Information Security Policy.
