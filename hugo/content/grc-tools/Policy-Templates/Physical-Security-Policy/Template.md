# Physical Security Policy

Policy Title: Physical Security Policy
Policy Number: PHY-001
Effective Date: ____
Version: 1.0
Classification: Internal
Approved By: ____

## Purpose

This policy establishes requirements to ensure that ____'s information assets, facilities, and Personnel are protected by physical controls that prevent unauthorized access, tampering, damage, or theft. Physical security is a foundational layer of the organization's defense-in-depth strategy; without it, technical controls can be bypassed entirely.

## Scope

This policy applies to all physical facilities owned, leased, or operated by ____, including offices, data centers, equipment rooms, and remote work locations where organizational assets are used or stored. It applies to all Personnel - employees, contractors, temporary workers, and visitors - who have physical access to organizational facilities or assets.

## Policy

### General Physical Security

- Physical access to ____ facilities is restricted to authorized Personnel and escorted visitors only.
- All Personnel must visibly wear organization-issued identification badges while in secure areas.
- Visitors and vendors must be escorted by an authorized employee at all times while on premises.
- A visitor log must be maintained including visitor name, organization, purpose, dates/times, host, and areas accessed.
- Visitor logs must be retained for a minimum of ____ (e.g., 12) months.
- Access records (badge reader logs, door access events) must be retained for a minimum of ____ (e.g., 12) months.

### Secure Areas

Locations housing sensitive information or critical infrastructure must meet enhanced physical security requirements:

- **Access Control:** Controlled by badge readers, biometric systems, or smart locks with unique credentials per individual.
- **Access Authorization:** Access is granted only to Personnel with a documented business need, approved by the area owner or Security Officer.
- **Access Review:** Access lists must be reviewed quarterly. Access for terminated Personnel must be revoked within 24 hours.
- **Locking:** Secure areas must be locked when unattended. Doors must not be propped open.
- **Lost or Stolen Credentials:** Must be reported immediately. Lost credential deactivated within ____ (e.g., 4) hours. Physical locks rekeyed within ____ (e.g., 7) days.
- **Camera Coverage:** Entry and exit points of secure areas should be covered by surveillance cameras with recording retention of at least ____ (e.g., 30) days.

### Clean Desk Policy

All Personnel must maintain a clean desk:
- Restricted and Confidential documents must be removed from desks and secured when unattended and at end of day.
- Computer workstations must be locked whenever the user steps away.
- Whiteboards containing Restricted or Confidential information must be erased after meetings.
- Printers, copiers, and fax machines must be cleared of sensitive documents immediately after use.
- Passwords, PINs, or other credentials must not be left in plain view.
- Keys, access badges, and physical credentials must not be left unattended.

### Workstation Security

- Workstations may only be used by authorized Personnel for job-related responsibilities.
- Personnel must report any unauthorized access attempts or suspicious activity.
- All workstations are organizational property and must be managed through the device management platform.
- Screens displaying sensitive information must not be visible to unauthorized individuals.
- Workstations should be logged off or shut down at end of day unless operational requirements dictate otherwise.

### Data Center and Server Room Security

For organizations that operate their own data centers or server rooms:

- Access must be limited to specifically authorized IT and facilities Personnel.
- Environmental controls must be monitored and alarmed (temperature, humidity, fire, water).
- UPS protection must be provided for critical systems. Backup generators should be available for extended outages.
- Emergency power-off switches must be clearly marked and placed near exits.
- Cabling must be organized, labeled, and protected.
- Third-party data center providers must hold recognized certifications (e.g., SOC 2, ISO 27001, SSAE 18).

### Off-Site Equipment

- Equipment may only be taken off-site for valid business reasons with documented authorization.
- The individual is responsible for the equipment's physical security at all times.
- Equipment must be tracked in the asset inventory (date of removal, expected return date, responsible individual).
- Equipment must not be left unattended in vehicles or public spaces.
- Lost or stolen off-site equipment must be reported immediately per the Incident Response Policy.

### Physical Security Perimeter

- Facility perimeters must be clearly defined (walls, fences, card-controlled entry points).
- External doors and ground-floor windows must be secured after business hours.
- Server rooms and high-security areas must be located away from public access points.
- Delivery and loading areas must be controlled to prevent unauthorized access.

### Alarm Systems

- Intrusion detection and alarm systems must be installed for facilities housing sensitive information or critical infrastructure.
- Alarm systems must be tested at least ____ (e.g., quarterly).
- Alarm monitoring must be provided by a 24/7 service or security operations center.
- Malfunctions must be investigated and remediated within ____ (e.g., 24) hours.

### Fire and Life Safety

- Fire extinguishers must be accessible, inspected, and maintained per local fire code.
- Emergency exit routes must be clearly marked and kept unobstructed.
- Evacuation procedures must be documented and communicated.
- Floor warden responsibilities must be assigned and trained.

## Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|----------------|
| ____ (e.g., CISO / Security Officer) | Policy owner; annual review; approve physical security exceptions |
| Facilities Management | Maintain building security systems (badge readers, locks, cameras, alarms) |
| IT / Infrastructure | Secure server rooms and data centers; manage environmental monitoring |
| Managers | Ensure team compliance with clean desk policy; report security incidents |
| All Personnel | Wear badges; secure workstations; maintain clean desk; escort visitors; report suspicious activity or lost credentials immediately |

## Compliance and Enforcement

Compliance is verified through physical walkthrough inspections (quarterly recommended), review of access and visitor logs, clean desk audits, camera footage review, and testing of alarm systems. Violations may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination.
