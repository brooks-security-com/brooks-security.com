10|## Purpose
11|
12|This Information Security Policy establishes the general framework for protecting information assets, minimizing the risk of information misuse, compromise, or loss, and ensuring compliance with legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations.
13|
14|## Scope
15|
16|This policy applies to:
17|
18|- Information in any form, regardless of the media on which it is stored, as well as any facility, system, or network used to store, process, or transfer information.
19|- All employees, temporary staff, contractors, vendors, suppliers, and any other person or entity (collectively "Personnel") that accesses the organization's networks, systems, or information.
20|- All activity while using or accessing the organization's information or information processing, storage, or transmission equipment, whether on premises or remotely.
21|- Information resources entrusted to the organization by external entities (customers, partners, and others).
22|- Documents, messages, and other communications created on or communicated via organizational systems, which are considered business records and subject to review in relation to audits, litigation, process improvement, and compliance.

42|## Information Security Objectives
43|
44|It is the policy of ____ that information, in all its forms - written, spoken, recorded electronically, or printed - will be protected from accidental or intentional unauthorized modification, destruction, or disclosure throughout its lifecycle. This protection includes an appropriate level of security over the equipment and software used to process, store, and transmit that information.
45|
46|The organization's information security objectives are:
47|
48|- **Confidentiality:** Data and information are protected from unauthorized access.
49|- **Integrity:** Data is intact, complete, and accurate.
50|- **Availability:** IT systems are available when needed.
51|
52|Additional objectives include:
53|
54|- Protecting information from all internal, external, deliberate, or accidental threats.
55|- Enabling secure information sharing.
56|- Encouraging consistent and professional use of information.
57|- Ensuring clarity about roles and responsibilities associated with protecting information.
58|- Ensuring business continuity and minimizing business damage.
59|- Protecting the organization from legal liability and the inappropriate use of information.

71|## Policy
72|
73|### Training
74|
75|All Personnel must complete information security awareness training upon hire and annually thereafter. Training must cover security policies, data handling, incident reporting, and role-specific responsibilities. Training records must be maintained.
76|
78|
79|### Clean Desk and Clear Screen
80|
81|All Personnel must maintain a clean desk and clear screen - sensitive materials must be secured when unattended, workstations must be locked when not in use, and passwords must never be left in accessible locations.
82|
84|
85|### Internet and Intranet Access
86|
87|Use of organizational computers, networks, and Internet access is a privilege granted by management and may be revoked at any time for inappropriate conduct, including but not limited to:
88|
89|- Sending chain letters or unsolicited commercial communications unrelated to legitimate business purposes.
90|- Engaging in private or personal business activities, including excessive use of instant messaging and chat rooms.
91|- Accessing networks, servers, drives, folders, or files without proper authorization.
92|- Making unauthorized copies of organizational data.
93|- Destroying, deleting, erasing, or concealing organizational data.
94|- Misrepresenting oneself or the organization.
95|- Violating applicable laws and regulations.
96|- Engaging in unlawful or malicious activities.
97|- Deliberately propagating malware or harmful code.
98|- Using abusive, profane, threatening, racist, sexist, or otherwise objectionable language.
99|- Sending, receiving, or accessing pornographic materials.
100|- Causing congestion, disruption, disablement, alteration, or impairment of organizational networks.
101|- Using recreational games on organizational equipment without authorization.
102|- Defeating or attempting to defeat security restrictions.
103|
104|Access will be discontinued upon termination of employment, completion of contract, end of service, or disciplinary action arising from violation of this policy. Inactive user accounts (no activity for ____ days) will be revoked. Privileges must be reevaluated by management annually.
105|
106|### Remote Work
107|
108|Secure remote access must be strictly controlled with encryption (e.g., VPNs) and strong authentication. Requirements include:
109|
110|- Personnel must protect their login credentials without exception.
111|- While using an organization-owned computer to remotely connect to the network, authorized users must ensure the remote host is not connected to any other network at the same time, with the exception of personal networks under their complete control.
112|- Current anti-malware software must be used on all computers.
113|- Equipment used to connect to organizational networks must meet the requirements for remote access and device use as stated in the Acceptable Use Policy, Asset Management Policy, and System Access Control Policy.
114|
115|Multi-factor authentication is required for all remote access tools. Remote access tools must support strong, end-to-end encryption. All security systems must not be disabled, interfered with, or circumvented.
116|
117|### Mobile Endpoint and Storage Devices
118|
119|Protecting endpoint devices issued by the organization or storing organizational data is the responsibility of every employee. This pertains to all devices that connect to the organizational network, regardless of ownership. Mobile endpoint and storage devices include desktop systems in remote work environments, laptops, mobile phones, USB devices, external drives, and any other mobile computing or storage device.
120|
121|**For endpoint devices:**
122|
123|- Organization-issued mobile devices must have anti-malware and endpoint security pre-installed.
124|- Users must run an online malware scanner at least once per ____.
125|- Mobile endpoint devices must meet the requirements in the Acceptable Use Policy and Asset Management Policy.
126|
127|**For storage devices:**
128|
129|- A risk analysis must be conducted prior to connection to the organizational network unless previously approved.
130|- Detection of incidents must be immediately reported.
131|- Stolen mobile devices must be immediately reported.
132|
133|### Intellectual Property Rights
134|
135|The organization takes handling and safeguarding of intellectual property seriously. Intellectual property rights include software or document copyright, design rights, trademarks, patents, and source code licenses.
136|
137|Procedures include:
138|
139|- Software must only be acquired through known and reputable sources to ensure copyright is not violated.
140|- An asset inventory will identify all assets with intellectual property protection requirements.
141|- Proof and evidence of ownership of licenses, master disks, manuals, etc. must be maintained.
142|- Review of the asset inventory must ensure only licensed products are installed.
143|- Compliance with terms and conditions for software and information obtained from public networks must be ensured.
144|
145|### Information Security Requirements Analysis
146|
147|The organization must identify information security requirements through methods including policies and regulations, threat modeling, incident reviews, and vulnerability thresholds. Factors to consider include:
148|
149|- Level of confidence required for user identity claims (authentication requirements).
150|- Access provisioning and authorization processes for business and privileged users.
151|- Informing users and operators of their duties and responsibilities.
152|- Protection needs of assets, particularly availability, confidentiality, and integrity.
153|- Business process requirements (logging, monitoring, non-repudiation).
154|- Other security controls (interfaces to logging, monitoring, or data leakage detection systems).
155|
156|### Employment Terms and Conditions
157|
158|Contractual obligations for safeguarding information include:
159|
160|- Signing a confidentiality or non-disclosure agreement prior to access to confidential information.
161|- Understanding legal responsibilities and rights, particularly concerning intellectual property.
162|- Responsibilities for classification of information and management of organizational assets.
163|- Responsibilities for handling information received from third parties.
164|- Reviewing and agreeing with organizational security policies.
165|- Duration of responsibilities beyond end of employment.
166|- Actions for non-compliance with terms, conditions, and security policies.
167|
168|### Disciplinary Process
169|
170|Policy violations must be addressed through a structured corrective action process. The organization reserves the right to combine or skip steps depending on the severity and circumstances of the violation. Behavior that is illegal (theft, violence, substance abuse, fraud) is grounds for immediate termination without progressive discipline.

All ISP policies must be reviewed, modified, or edited annually to meet necessary security standards. All policies must be approved by authorized personnel.

186|## Compliance and Enforcement
187|
188|Management, under the explicit authority granted by ____, retains the authority and responsibility to monitor and enforce compliance with this Policy and other policies, standards, procedures, and guidelines.
