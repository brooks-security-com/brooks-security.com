10|## Overview
11|
12|____ is committed to maintaining a robust internal control environment to ensure the effectiveness and efficiency of operations, the reliability of reporting, and compliance with applicable laws, regulations, and industry frameworks. To this end, ____ performs regular control self-assessments to evaluate the adequacy and operating effectiveness of its internal controls.
13|
14|## Purpose
15|
16|This process establishes the requirements for conducting self-assessments of internal controls within ____. The assessments help identify control weaknesses, gaps, or potential improvements, ensure compliance with applicable framework commitments (e.g., SOC 2, ISO 27001, NIST 800-53, PCI DSS, HIPAA), and provide evidence for internal and external audits.
17|
18|## Scope
19|
20|This process applies to all internal controls and processes that are subject to compliance framework reporting at ____. This includes:
21|
22|- Controls covering security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy (SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria).
23|- Controls mapped to applicable regulatory requirements.
24|- Controls documented in the organization's control framework or GRC platform.
25|
26|## Responsibilities
27|
28|| Role | Responsibility |
29||------|----------------|
30|| ____ (e.g., Security Officer / Compliance Officer) | Oversees the control self-assessment process; schedules assessments; reviews findings and corrective actions; reports to executive management |
31|| Department Heads / Control Owners | Conduct assessments within their respective areas; provide evidence of control effectiveness; address identified control deficiencies |
32|| Internal Audit / GRC Team | Provides support and oversight to ensure self-assessments are conducted in accordance with this process; assists in evaluating effectiveness of corrective actions |
33|| Executive Management | Reviews overall assessment outcomes; approves significant control changes or resource allocations |
34|
35|## Assessment Cadence
36|
37|Control self-assessments must be conducted at the following frequency:
38|
39|- **Annual Comprehensive Assessment:** All in-scope controls assessed at least once per year.
40|- **Quarterly High-Risk Control Assessment:** Controls that address high-risk areas, have a history of deficiencies, or are critical to compliance must be assessed quarterly.
41|- **Event-Driven Assessment:** Controls are re-assessed upon:
42| - Significant changes to the control environment (system changes, process changes, organizational restructuring).
43| - Discovery of a control failure or incident related to the control.
44| - Changes in applicable regulations or compliance framework requirements.
45| - Findings from internal or external audits indicating control weakness.
46|
47|## Assessment Execution Requirements
48|
49|Control self-assessments must be conducted according to a documented, repeatable process that includes planning, evidence collection, effectiveness evaluation against the defined rating scale (Operating Effectively, Needs Improvement, Not Effective), reporting of findings with severity classification, corrective action planning with assigned owners and target dates, independent verification of remediation, and formal assessment close-out with a final report to the Security Oversight Committee. All assessment documentation and evidence must be retained for a minimum of ____ years in a secure, access-controlled repository. The assessment process is reviewed annually for continued relevance and effectiveness.
